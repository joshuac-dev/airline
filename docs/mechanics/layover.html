Layover

A layover is when a passenger takes a flight, then takes another flight after they arrive to get to their destination. Passengers generally do this because there's no direct flight to where they want to go.

Costs

There are hidden costs that passengers pay when taking a transit flight.

First, an extra

$100

is added to the price for normal connections, reduced to

$25

for switching between alliance members and yourself. This is part of what makes alliances so strong.

Next, an additional time cost is imposed on connections where neither of the flights have

more than 42

frequency, equal to

420 / frequency of the more frequent flight

.

For example, say someone from Xuzhou wants to go to Oslo for some weird reason, and decides to take a (now unavailable) Xuzhou -> Stockholm -> Oslo flight on Golden Sun. (This example is old)

Xuzhou -> Stockholm costs

$1000

on Fly or die, and Stockholm -> Oslo costs

$150

on Tropic Direct, making it a total of

$1150

.

First, we add the

$25

fee between alliance members.

Next, we add the additional time cost. The larger flight has 35 frequency, while the smaller one only has 1 frequency, but we only count the larger flight.

420 / 35 = $12

So the total cost that passengers see is

$1000 + $150 + $25 + $12 = $1187

Now another example. Let's say this Xuzhou guy wants to fly on top quality service from Union Airlines on the Stockholm -> Oslo leg. The

$100

penalty for transiting, the

$180

that Union Airlines charges for its tickets, and the

$20

time cost means it'll cost

$1000 + $180 + $100 + $20 = $1300

Costs a whole

$113 more

from the perspective of the passenger when the price difference is only **$30**.

**Exceptions to the formula:**

Budget pax see added connection cost as only

half

the actual added cost, Swift pax see it as

2x

the actual added cost. For example, if transiting to a new alliance, which usually costs an

extra $100

, Budget pax will add on only

$50

while Swift pax will add on

$200

.
