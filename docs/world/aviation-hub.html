Aviation Hub

In v2.2, released August 23, 2025, the new aviation hub was added. Details below, summarized by Gemini.

1. Basic Requirements to be a Hub:

  • Enough Passengers: An airport must have at least 10,000 passengers. If it has fewer, its hub strength is 0.

2. Calculating the Hub Strength:

  • The "Ratio" Score: The code looks at the airport's total number of passengers ( pax ) compared to the number of people who want to fly directly from that airport ( directDemand ).
    • If there's no direct demand ( directDemand is 0), the ratio score is a fixed value of 20. This is a special case to give airports with no direct flights a chance to be a hub.
    • If there is direct demand , the code calculates the ratio: total passengers / direct demand .
      • If this ratio is less than 2, the hub strength is 0. This means the airport is mainly serving people flying directly, not connecting passengers.
      • If the ratio is 2 or more, the code uses a mathematical formula (a logarithm) to calculate the ratio score. This formula rewards airports with a very high ratio (meaning they have lots of connecting passengers) but caps the score at 20.
      • The "Passenger" score is calculated by taking the airport's passenger count, but capping it at 500,000, and then dividing it by 75,000. This means larger airports get a bigger boost to their hub strength, but it doesn't increase infinitely.

      What the Hub Strength Does:

      For every point of "Aviation Hub Strength" an airport has:

      • It increases the airport's reputation by 0.25.
      • It increases the population it serves by 20,000.
      • It increases the airport's income by $100.

      Direct demand

      You can use the passenger score of airports, for airports without aviation hub, to calculatw their exact total direct demand. This is equal to required passengers / 2, if the requirement > 10,000 passengers.
