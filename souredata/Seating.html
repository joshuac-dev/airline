Seating

Airplanes can be divided into 3 categories of seating. Economy (or abbreviated as "Y"), Business (J), and First Class (F). These are important when looking at demand.

Configuration

To change the seating arrangement of an airplane you already own, select the "Airplane" button from the left hover-menu, select the airplane model that you wish to edit, and click the "Config" button in the airplane details panel on the right side. Click "Add New Configuration" or make changes to an existing configuration. Each model of airplane can support a maximum of 5 different configurations.

You can use the padlock button to lock-in a category of seating, allowing you to make changes to a different category without overwriting the changes you've already made. If it is not locked, the other two classes will conform, erasing your changes.

When you are happy with the seating arrangement, click the green checkmark button to save your changes.

The star button will set the default configuration, which will be automatically applied to any new plane unless you change it.

To delete a config, press the red "x" button. You can not delete your default configuration. The original default configuration is an all-economy layout.

You may click and drag any of your existing airplane icons between your saved configurations to change their seating arrangement on the fly.

Capacity to Seats

The three seating categories each take up different amounts of capacity. The conversion is shown below:

Space of Seats in a Configuration
Seats Capacity
Economy (Y) 1 1
Business (J) 1 2.5
First (F) 1 6

If you have 1 business seat and the rest are all economy, you waste .5 of a capacity.

To make higher profits off the J and F class pax, make sure you try to make your prices reasonable higher, otherwise, it is not as profitable.
